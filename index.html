About

I design products people actually use — and I can explain why each decision matters.

I am finishing a Master's in Computer Science at Instituto Superior Técnico, focused on interaction design and UX research. I love turning messy user problems into clear product decisions.

My thesis work combines heuristic evaluation, task performance, and perceived usability. In practice, that means I do not stop at "this looks better"; I focus on whether the change helps users complete real tasks with less effort.

I can also ship frontend when needed, but my main value is product thinking: understanding constraints, prioritizing the right problems, and connecting design choices to measurable outcomes. Based in Lisbon, open to remote and relocation.

nextflat CH

A live Swiss real estate platform (500+ users) that had grown without a unified design language. I joined as the sole designer during an active growth phase — responsible for identifying UX problems, redesigning core surfaces, and building the component system that made future work sustainable.

  • schemaRedesigned: homepage, property feed, and user dashboards across 2 release cycles
  • insightsImpact: ~30% reduction in estimated frontend implementation time (based on engineering estimates before and after the design system was in place)

Featured Case Study 02

SageBook

Most cooking apps are recipe databases. This one is built around the moment people actually fail — mid-recipe, not at the planning stage. Over 60% of new cooks reported abandoning recipes while actively cooking; the design targets that exact friction point.

  • manage_searchResearch: survey (60%+ mid-recipe abandonment finding) + 2 rounds of usability testing
  • schemaCore decision: step-by-step execution flow with contextual ingredients and progress clarity
  • insightsTesting: task timing across usability sessions showed faster setup completion and zero backtracking in v2 vs. 3/5 participants hitting dead ends in v1

Research & Method

My process is simple: define the user problem, design the smallest decision that solves it, and track at least one outcome metric (measured or clearly estimated). That is how I frame every project in this portfolio.
