About Me

I'm a UX Designer and Frontend Developer with a passion for creating interfaces that are not only beautiful but also functional and accessible. With a background in computer science, I bring a unique blend of technical knowledge and design sensibility to every project.

My approach to design is user-centered, focusing on understanding the people I'm designing for and the problems they're trying to solve. I believe that the best digital experiences are those that feel intuitive and natural, almost invisible to the user.

When I'm not designing or coding, you can find me exploring new design trends, attending tech meetups, or sketching in my notebook at a local café.

My Design Philosophy

I believe in human-centered design that solves real problems while creating delightful experiences. Hover over each segment of the wheel to learn more about my design process.

Each phase is crucial and informs the others in a continuous cycle of improvement.

1. Research

I begin every project with thorough user research to understand needs, pain points, and behaviors. This includes interviews, surveys, competitive analysis, and market research.

Tools: User interviews, Surveys, Personas, Journey maps

Example: For ReMi app, I interviewed 12 recent movers to understand their challenges.

2. Ideate

I explore multiple solutions through sketching, brainstorming, and conceptual mapping before settling on a direction. This phase is about quantity of ideas over quality.

Tools: Mind maps, Sketching, Crazy 8s, How Might We

Example: For Clicky Companion, I ideated 20+ different interactions to make mood tracking engaging.

3. Design

I create intuitive, accessible interfaces with a focus on visual hierarchy, consistency, and delight. Each design choice is made deliberately with the user's needs in mind.

Tools: Wireframing, Prototyping, Visual design, Interaction design

Example: SageBook's cottagecore aesthetic was chosen to create a warm, inviting learning environment.

4. Test & Iterate

I continuously test with users and refine designs based on feedback and data. No design is perfect on the first try, and user insights drive improvements.

Tools: Usability testing, A/B testing, Analytics, Heatmaps

Example: ReMi's onboarding flow was redesigned 3 times based on usability test findings.
